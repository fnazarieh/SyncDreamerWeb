Abstract

Generating realistic and expressive talking avatars from minimal input remains a central challenge in digital human synthesis. We introduce SyncDreamer, a diffusion-based framework that learns synchronized, semantically grounded audio–visual generation of expressive avatars. Our model integrates an audio dynamics encoder, a visual adapter, and a text-conditioned diffusion process to produce temporally coherent, controllable facial motion while preserving identity under diverse poses and expressions.

Experiments demonstrate state-of-the-art performance in expressivity and identity consistency, enabling new possibilities in virtual communication, entertainment, and personalized digital avatars.

Results and Demonstrations

Expressive Avatar Generation

SyncDreamer produces expressive talking avatars from a single reference image and speech audio, capturing emotion and natural head motion while maintaining identity.

Robustness to Audio Variability

SyncDreamer demonstrates strong robustness to variations in audio characteristics such as rhythm, energy, and speaker identity. Using the same reference image, our model produces synchronized and expressive motion that adapts naturally to each unique input voice.
